A ghost is believed to be the lingering spirit or soul ​of a dead person that has not quite moved on to the ​afterlife. Ghosts have been interpreted as anything ​from malevolent phantoms to benevolent chaperons ​throughout history and across cultural boundaries.

A vampire is a mythical creature typically depicted as ​a reanimated corpse that is believed to come out of ​graves at night and suck the blood of living people.​These critters are considered as immortal beings, ​only susceptible to sunlight or Christian implements.

Frankenstein is a novel written by Mary Shelley over ​200 years ago. It is about a sapient monster ​produced by a young scientist Victor Frankenstein.​The name "Frankenstein" has often been misused to ​refer to the monster itself rather than to his creator.

A witch is interpreted as an old woman in a pointed ​hat and a black cloak who practices witchcraft, ​exercises magic and makes potions. Witches possess ​supernatural powers of bewitching people by casting ​spells and flying in the sky with broomsticks.

An Irish trickster nicknamed Stingy Jack was ​condemned to haunt the countryside upon his passing ​with only a hollowed-out turnip illuminated by a ​blazing coal to light his way. Pumpkins have replaced ​turnips when Irish immigrants moved to America.
